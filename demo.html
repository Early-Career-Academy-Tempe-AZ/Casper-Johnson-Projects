Back Story

In a land, known only to myth and legend, the world has three guardians, the Guardians of Light.

The Sky, vast, stretching higher and farther than any creature could travel.
The Sun, bright and blazing, providing the warmth of life to the creatures of the land, and burning away the shadows.
The Moon, providing balance, the benign night to the sun's day, the pull and push of the waves, acting as an equal and opposite force.
These three entities represented the forces of light, and brought life together. This peace could not last long, for without pain and suffering there would be no value to life. Thus, once every thousand years, the Sky was clouded by darkness, the Sun eclipsed blotted out by shadow, and the Moon eclipsed by evil, replaced by a red light, shining alone in the eternally night sky. Great misfortune was brought to the creatures of the land during these times. The trees of the forest slowly withered, leaving the land scared and the life empty. Many died of hunger, dehydration, or rampant illness. But this would not be the end. For in the last moments of the Sky, the Sun, and the Moon's life, they gifted to the forest a child. This child would grow to be the savior of the land, and piece together the broken fragments of the Sky, the Sun, and the Moon, creating them anew and vanquishing the evil. If this child were to survive to fulfil it. And so, not wanting to lose its control, the darkness sent its forces forth into the world to revenge the forest's remains in search of the child. This was the legend, retold from generation to generation, and told to the child who would become a mighty hero.
